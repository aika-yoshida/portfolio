## Overall Impression

The underlying system is disciplined and the copy is genuinely good — this doesn't read as AI slop, and the bespoke per-case CSS motifs are a real, confident design decision. But none of that matters if the page's actual first five seconds are an unexplained password wall, and it costs real credibility that three of the four "Other Works" cards and the About portrait currently render as broken/blank because `assets/` is empty. The single biggest opportunity: fix the two access-blocking defects (gate, missing images) before anything else — everything else on this page is a refinement, those two are the difference between "recruiter reads this" and "recruiter never sees it."

## What's Working

- **Bespoke CSS motifs per case study** instead of a repeated icon-grid template — each one (urgency triage, architecture, emergency branching, party/home duality) visually encodes the actual insight of that case, and works without depending on photography that might not exist.
- **Honest capability disclosure**: the `.crow` rows explicitly separate `実務経験` from `学習・実践中` rather than blurring the two — this directly executes PRODUCT.md's stated positioning and reads as a real credibility mechanic, not a hedge.
- **Copy quality**: specific, confident lines with no padding or generic AI-portfolio phrasing — matches this repo's own writing guidelines rather than reading like generated copy.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Password gate blocks 100% of traffic with no explanation, and is a permanent dead end without JS.** `#gate` covers the entire viewport with no visible content behind it — not even the hero — until the correct password is entered, with no "why is this protected" text and no request-access path. `SITE_PASSWORD` sits in plaintext in a `<script>` tag, so it stops nobody technical while blocking the actual non-technical recruiter audience. Only the JS submit handler calls `gate.remove()`, so if JavaScript fails for any reason (corporate script-blocking, ad blocker), the gate never clears — a true dead end. This directly undercuts PRODUCT.md's own stated goal of recruiters "forwarding internally": a forwarded recipient has no password and no way in.
**Why it matters**: The entire site exists to get a recruiter to email her; a gate with no context or fallback can zero out that outcome before the hero is even visible.
**Fix**: Remove the gate from the recruiter-facing homepage, or at minimum add a one-line explanation plus a mailto fallback ("Recruiter? Email me for access") directly on the gate screen, and make the gate degrade gracefully (visible, non-blocking) if JS doesn't run.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden` (or `/impeccable onboard` if the gate itself is being redesigned as a real access-request flow).

**[P0] `assets/` is currently empty — three "Other Works" cards and the About portrait are broken right now.** Verified directly against the filesystem: zero files in `assets/`. `autooasis.png`, `tiktok-hollywoodreporter.png`, `digsy-lp.png`, and `portrait.jpg` all 404 and fall through to the `onerror` handlers, degrading three photo-dependent cards to flat colored rectangles with only a small badge label, and collapsing the About avatar to text initials. This lands right after four strong Featured Works cards, at the exact moment the page needs to sustain momentum into About/Contact.
**Why it matters**: This is the literal failure brand.md calls out by name — "a solid-color rectangle where a hero image should go is worse than a representative stock photo... zero images is a bug, not a design choice." A recruiter mid-skim hits a visibly broken section right when credibility should be peaking.
**Fix**: Add the real screenshots before this ships, or temporarily extend the same custom-CSS-motif treatment already proven on the Featured cards to these three so nothing ships visibly broken.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden` for the fallback/empty-state handling; asset production itself is outside any impeccable command's scope.

**[P1] The skip link bypasses the hero pitch for screen-reader users.** `<a class="skip" href="#work">本文へスキップ</a>` jumps straight past the entire hero section — including "課題を解くことから、体験の設計へ。" and the positioning paragraph PRODUCT.md calls "the line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds" — straight to the Works grid. A screen-reader user who reflexively activates the skip link, standard behavior, never hears the pitch at all.
**Why it matters**: The one line this whole site is built to land never reaches an entire class of visitors, undermining both the accessibility baseline PRODUCT.md commits to (WCAG AA) and the positioning itself.
**Fix**: Point the skip link at the hero (or wrap hero+content in `<main>` and target that) instead of jumping past it.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable audit` (accessibility pass).

**[P2] Confirmed design-token drift, plus an undocumented "muted-on-dark" color gap.** The detector found 9 radius values and 24 font-size values outside DESIGN.md's documented scales (e.g., a 22px card radius against the documented 26/18/12px scale; 13px/12.5px/15px scattered instead of consolidating), independently verified as genuine drift, not false positives. Separately, several dark-panel text colors (used on `.vis`/`.contact-panel`, where the light-background `--mute`/`--line` tokens wouldn't have enough contrast) were never catalogued in DESIGN.md at all — a real documentation gap, not a code defect.
**Why it matters**: Small inconsistencies compound; every undocumented one-off makes the next redesign or case-study page more likely to drift further from "The Case File" system DESIGN.md just codified.
**Fix**: Consolidate the font-size/radius values onto the documented scale where there's no functional reason for the exception, and add a documented dark-panel muted-text token pair to DESIGN.md for the ones that are legitimate.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish` (or `/impeccable document` to refresh DESIGN.md with the missing dark-panel tokens first).

**[P2] Mobile hides the primary CTA entirely.** `.topbar .cta{display:none}` at `max-width:640px` removes the persistent "Get in touch" pill on mobile — the nav's "Contact" text link still exists, but the highest-visibility, always-present conversion affordance disappears specifically on the platform PRODUCT.md flags some recruiters using first ("some will click through on mobile first").
**Why it matters**: The one persistent, high-contrast conversion path is the one thing removed on mobile, for an audience explicitly named as sometimes-mobile-first.
**Fix**: Keep a compact mail icon/pill in the mobile topbar instead of hiding the CTA outright.
**Suggested command**: `/impeccable adapt`.

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer)**: Hits the password gate first, with zero context for why it exists or how to get in — the worst possible first five seconds for anyone unfamiliar with the link. Once past the gate (if they get past it), nav is clearly text-labeled and "Read case study →" is unambiguous — Jordan does fine after the gate; the gate itself is the abandonment point.

**Riley (Stress Tester)**: Found two real defects by probing edges: (1) with JavaScript disabled or blocked, the gate can never be dismissed — only the JS submit handler removes it, producing a permanent dead end with no fallback; (2) the empty `assets/` folder means three "Other Works" cards silently degrade to blank gray rectangles rather than failing loudly or gracefully.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent)**: Mixed. Strong fundamentals — `aria-hidden="true"` correctly applied to every decorative CSS motif, consistent `:focus-visible` outlines project-wide, specific (non-generic) alt text on real images, and a properly-labeled `role="dialog" aria-modal="true"` on the gate with focus correctly sent to the password input. But the skip link (P1 above) actively removes the hero from Sam's experience, and broken images mean no visual signal at all for 3 of 4 Other Works projects — an `img` set to `display:none` on error is removed from the accessibility tree entirely, not just hidden visually.

**The time-pressed recruiter (project-specific)**: This persona's entire task — skim 2-3 case studies, decide whether to email — cannot start at all if the link was forwarded without a password, directly conflicting with PRODUCT.md's own "forward internally" scenario. Once past the gate, the persistent sticky topbar and clear anchor nav serve this persona well. But hitting the broken Other Works grid mid-skim, right after being impressed by four detailed case studies, is exactly the kind of small credibility ding that could tip a marginal "maybe" into a pass in a 90-second read.

- `--mute` (#6f6a61) on `--page` (#f5f3ee) computes to roughly 4.84:1 — passes WCAG AA but only barely; worth confirming with an actual contrast tool rather than treating it as safely settled.
- The purple radial-glow motif repeats identically, unchanged, across three separate dark panels (`.feature .vis::after`, `.fullbleed::before`, `.contact-panel::before`) — sanctioned by DESIGN.md as the one allowed gradient-adjacent effect, but three unchanged repeats starts to read as a template tic rather than a bespoke touch.
- `.feature .body h4` headings use hard `<br>` line breaks at a fixed 18.5px; on narrow viewports the intended two-line break can soft-wrap into three or four lines since nothing constrains it — not an overflow bug, just messier rhythm than designed around 320-375px widths.
- 8 total clickable case-study entries (4 Featured + 4 Other) sit at the edge of the Working Memory Rule's "overloaded" threshold, with only position/visual-treatment — not an explicit cue — indicating where a time-pressed reader should start.
- `crow:hover .num{color:accent}` is a nice subtle hover reward on capabilities rows but has no touch-device equivalent.
